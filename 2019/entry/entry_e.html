Contest sign-up information

Rules for participation

  • Please confirm the following rules, and you can entry this contest from 【How to sign-up】section below.
  • The contest is held as a team competition.
  • There is no restriction on the number of team members.
  • A team can have members from different organizations.
  • A team may participate in the contest without disclosing their organizations or personal names.
  • A team may only participate a preliminary round or final round.
  • A team member cannot join multiple teams.
  • Each team must have a representative; the representative does not have to be a team member.
    • A student representative is not allowed; ask your laboratory instructors to become the representative
    • The same person can become representatives of multiple teams.
  • There is no restriction on the software or hardware used in the contest.
  • At least one member from a team must register to CSS. The member must make a presentation at the PWS Cup session and later submit their presentation materials. If this is difficult for some reasons, please contact the PWS Cup organizing committee.

Contest overview

In this contest, you are to anonymize traces (i.e., movement history data that is a set of location data) while balancing the utility and privacy of the anonymized traces.

There are two phases in the contest.

In the data anonymizing phase, you are to anonymize traces so as to make the traces resistant to ID disclosure attack (i.e., re-identification of the original user) and trace inference attack (i.e., restoring the original location data). You also need to try to preserve the characteristics of the original traces as much as you can when you anonymize them.

In the ID disclosure & trace inference phase, you are to assess anonymized traces submitted by other participants. You are expected to assess the privacy level of the anonymized traces by attempting to re-identify the original users and to restore the original location data in the traces.
